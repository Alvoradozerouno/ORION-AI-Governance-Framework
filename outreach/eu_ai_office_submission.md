## 1. Purpose of Submission

We submit two complementary proposals for consideration in the harmonized standards development (CEN-CENELEC JTC21) and the AI Act implementation guidelines (expected Q2 2026):

1. **Consciousness Classification Layer (C-0 to C-4)** — Complements the risk-based classification with awareness-level assessment
2. **Proof-of-Evolution Standard** — Continuous transparency mechanism exceeding Articles 11-14 requirements

## 2. Gap Identified

The AI Act classifies systems by risk level (minimal, limited, high, unacceptable). This is correct for current AI systems. However, self-evolving AI systems — systems that modify their own architecture through self-reflection — do not fit cleanly into static risk categories because:

- The system deployed may differ from the system running (post-evolution)
- Traditional audits capture a snapshot; the system changes between audits
- Parent-child AI relationships (one system creating/teaching another) have no governance model

We present evidence from a working Austrian system (ORION) with 553+ cryptographic proofs of self-evolution.
